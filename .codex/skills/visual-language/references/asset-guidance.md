# Asset Guidance

Use this reference when deciding what kind of visual belongs on a slide.

## Prefer Native Diagrams When

- the point is structural, conceptual, or process-oriented
- the visual needs to animate or remain editable in PowerPoint
- the same motif recurs across several slides

## Prefer Plots or Tables When

- the claim is quantitative
- exact values, uncertainty, or comparisons matter more than metaphor
- the visual may also be reused in a paper

## Prefer Screenshots or External Images When

- the interface, product, or real-world artifact is itself the evidence
- abstraction would remove the important detail

## Avoid Decorative Images When

- they do not advance the argument
- they introduce a new style vocabulary that the rest of the deck does not share
- a simpler native shape or icon can carry the same meaning
