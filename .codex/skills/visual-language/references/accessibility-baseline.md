# Accessibility Baseline

Accessibility is part of the visual system, not a late cleanup step.

## Minimum Rules

- use readable font sizes for the back of the room; avoid tiny captions that only work on a laptop screen
- keep strong foreground/background contrast
- do not rely on color alone to encode meaning
- ensure every slide has a clear title or equivalent orienting label
- make chart takeaways readable in words, not only in visual encodings
- keep reading order aligned with spatial order and reveal order

## Chart And Diagram Rules

- pair color coding with labels, shapes, or line styles where possible
- call out the key comparison in text when a chart matters to the argument
- avoid dense legends when direct labels are possible
- ensure important connectors and states remain distinguishable in grayscale or poor projection conditions

## Image Rules

- screenshots and photos should be cropped and annotated so the audience knows where to look
- if an image needs a long spoken explanation before it becomes understandable, simplify it or replace it

## Design Intent

A visually impressive slide that excludes part of the room is not a successful slide.
