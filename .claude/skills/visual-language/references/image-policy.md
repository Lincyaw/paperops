# Image Policy

Choose image types by argumentative function, not by visual novelty.

## Use Native Diagrams When

- the point is structural, conceptual, relational, or process-oriented
- the visual should animate or remain editable in PowerPoint
- the same motif recurs across several slides

## Use Plots Or Tables When

- the claim is quantitative
- exact values, uncertainty, ranking, or comparison matter more than metaphor
- the figure may also be reused in a paper or report

## Use Screenshots When

- the interface or real artifact is itself the evidence
- the screenshot is being interpreted, not just used as decoration
- annotations can direct attention to the relevant part quickly

## Use Photos Sparingly When

- the real-world object or context is necessary for audience understanding
- a schematic abstraction would hide the important evidence
- the photo supports the claim rather than substituting for it

## Avoid Decorative Images When

- they do not advance the argument
- they introduce a different style vocabulary from the rest of the deck
- they consume focal attention that should belong to the message
- a simpler diagram, icon, or plot can carry the same meaning

## Default Rule

When in doubt, prefer the most editable visual form that still communicates the point clearly.
